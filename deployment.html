What is Deployment?

Deployment is the final stage of mobile app development where your application is prepared, packaged, and released to app stores such as Google Play Store and Apple App Store so users can download and use it.

Key Steps in Deployment

  • Final Testing: Ensure the app is stable, secure, and bug-free.
  • Build Generation: Create release builds (APK/AAB for Android, IPA for iOS).
  • App Store Assets: Prepare app name, description, screenshots, icons, and privacy policy.
  • Store Submission: Upload the app and complete store listing details.
  • Review Process: App stores review your app before publishing.

Android App Deployment

  • Uses Android App Bundle (AAB) or APK format
  • Uploaded via Google Play Console
  • Supports internal, closed, and open testing tracks
  • Review process is usually fast (hours to days)

iOS App Deployment

  • Uses IPA format
  • Uploaded using Xcode or Transporter
  • Managed through App Store Connect
  • Requires Apple review (can take several days)

Post-Deployment Tasks

  • Monitor crashes and performance
  • Read user feedback and reviews
  • Release updates and bug fixes
  • Improve features based on user needs

Why Deployment Matters

A smooth deployment ensures your app reaches users reliably, maintains quality standards, and builds trust through secure and consistent updates.
